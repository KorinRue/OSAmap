New York Harbor Water Quality Results
This map shows 2015 water quality results from NYC Department of Environmental Protection Harbor Survey and from NYC Water Trail Association , a group of citizen scientists that test water in the harbor during the recreation season.

Use the time slider below to see water quality results throughout the year. Below the time slider is a chart of precipitation over the year.

Water Quality Standards for Swimming

Acceptable: less than 35 MPN
Unacceptable if levels persist: 35-104 MPN
Unacceptable: more than 104 MPN

MPN = most probable number. This is the count of Enterococcus for a 100 milliliter water sample. Enterococcus is a bacteria most commonly found in feces of warm-blooded animals (like humans!). They are tested for to indicate sewage or other fecal pollution that could contain pathogens that could make us sick if we fish, swim or boat in the water.
