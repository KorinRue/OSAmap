New York City CSO - Combined Sewer Overflow
This map shows Combined Sewer Outfalls and their drainage areas. Most of New York City, about 70%, has a combined sewer system. This means stormwater and sanitary wastewater are conveyed to a wastewater treatment plant through the same system. During rainstorms, combined sewer systems can become inundated with stormwater which could overload the capacity of the wastewater treatment plant. When this happens, the mixture of raw sewage and stormwater runoff is diverted to the nearest water body through a combined sewer outfall.

These events are called CSO - Combined Sewer Overflows.

This map is a project of Open Sewer Atlas NYC through the support of NYC Soil & Water Conservation District. All data are sourced from the NYC Department of Environmental Protection.
